Why Vacuum Thermoforming?

If your part is large, your volumes are low-to-mid, and your timeline is tight — thermoforming usually wins. Here's why.

The Process

  1. Heat plastic sheet heated to forming temperature
  2. Form vacuum draws the sheet onto a single-sided mold
  3. Cool part solidifies on the mold
  4. Trim robotic spindle trimming to final geometry
Vacuum Thermoforming vs Injection Molding
Criterion Vacuum Thermoforming Injection Molding
Tooling cost Single-sided mold — a fraction of the investment Matched steel tooling — high upfront cost
Tooling lead time Typically 2–3 months Typically 3–6 months, depending on size & complexity
Part size Excellent for large panels & deep draws Limited by press size; large parts get very expensive
Volume sweet spot Low to mid volume Very high volume
Design changes Fast, low-cost tool modification Expensive and slow to modify
Per-part economics Amortized tooling keeps total cost-per-part lower at low/mid volume Wins only when volume is high enough to absorb tooling

At low-to-mid volumes, amortized tooling cost dominates part economics. That's where thermoforming delivers the lowest total cost — without compromising on automotive-grade quality.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does Sathien NVH (SNVH) manufacture?

SNVH is a Tier-1 automotive supplier specializing in large-format vacuum thermoforming — producing thermoformed interior and NVH components on a build-to-print basis under IATF 16949 quality systems.

Where is SNVH located?

SNVH operates from Nong Suea, Pathum Thani, Thailand, on Liab Klong 12 Road — north of Bangkok, within Thailand's automotive manufacturing cluster.

When is vacuum thermoforming a better choice than injection molding?

Vacuum thermoforming is usually the better choice for large parts at low-to-mid volumes. Its single-sided tooling costs far less and is ready in about 2–3 months, versus 3–6 months for injection tooling, so amortized tooling keeps the total cost-per-part lower at those volumes.

Is SNVH certified?

Yes. SNVH is certified to IATF 16949 for automotive quality management and ISO 14001 for environmental management.

Can SNVH manufacture parts from our drawings?

Yes. SNVH works build-to-print — send drawings or CAD and SNVH handles process design, tooling coordination, PPAP submission and serial production to your specification.

Does SNVH supply industries other than automotive?

Yes. While automotive is SNVH's proven Tier-1 base, the same thermoforming and quality capabilities are open to home appliances, construction, agriculture and industrial packaging.
